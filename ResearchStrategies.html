University Research Policy

Fostering Innovation, Integrity, and Collaboration

Research Policy Overview

A University Research Policy provides a comprehensive framework that governs the conduct of research within an academic institution. It ensures that all research activities are carried out in compliance with ethical principles, legal regulations, and the university's overarching goals. The policy plays a vital role in upholding research integrity, fostering innovation, and facilitating collaboration, while ensuring accountability and transparency in research practices.

Key Components

One of the primary components of a university research policy is the emphasis on research ethics and integrity. This aspect ensures that all research is conducted responsibly and ethically, respecting the rights of human and animal subjects where applicable. Ethical review processes, often overseen by Institutional Review Boards (IRBs) or ethics committees, are required to assess research proposals involving sensitive topics to safeguard the well-being of participants and uphold public trust in research outcomes.

Intellectual property (IP) and ownership is another key focus of research policy. The University establish clear guidelines regarding the ownership of discoveries, inventions, and creative works resulting from research. It involves determining whether the intellectual property belongs to the researcher, the university, or an external funding body. Additionally, the policy outlines procedures for the protection of IP through patents, trademarks, or copyrights, and for commercialization through technology transfer offices or licensing agreements.

For detailed University research policy, please contact the university administration.

Research Themes

In the Somali context, social sciences research themes often intersect with issues of political instability, social cohesion, economic development, and cultural identity. Key research themes include:

  • Mental Health
  • Conflict, Peacebuilding, and State Reconstruction
  • Social Inequality and Marginalization
  • Gender Roles, Empowerment, and Women's Rights
  • Children/Youth, Education, and Employment
  • Migration, Displacement, and Refugees
